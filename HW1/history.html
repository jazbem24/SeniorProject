The History of Portland Rap

Portland Hip-Hop has come a long way, but it still has so far to go, a lot like the city of Portland. Many would not expect that Portland doesn't have a hip-hop/rap scene, but alas there is. It has just recently started to thrive with the emergence of hip-hop culture shops and galleries, Rap/hip-hop showcases, and artists that are starting to become popular, especially with the role that the internet now plays in the global music scene. The scene has gone through a lot, especially with the roles that gentrification has tested the culture. Some of the ways that gentrification and discrimination has threatened Portland hip-hop is listed below:
  1. Many hip-hop venues in the city have been shut down permanentlyby the Portland police
  2. Over displays of authority, including gang enforcement officers, enforcing color dress codes in minority neighborhoods
  3. Minorities and musical talent have been pushed further and further out of the city
